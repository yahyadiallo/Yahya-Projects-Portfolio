# Seattle Housing Affordability Analysis

## Overview

This project explores housing affordability trends in Seattle by comparing apartment rent growth and median household income growth from 2018 to 2023. The goal was to investigate whether income growth has kept up with rising housing costs before, during, and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

The visualization combines multiple public datasets and presents the findings through an interactive D3.js line chart that highlights changes over time and differences between rent and income growth.

---

## Research Question

**Have household incomes in Seattle increased fast enough to keep up with rising housing costs?**

Housing affordability has become a major concern for Seattle residents. This project examines how apartment rents and household incomes changed over a six-year period and whether income growth was sufficient to cancel out increasing housing costs.

## Datasets

### Apartment Rent Data
Seattle Open Data – Apartment Market Rent Prices by Census Tract: https://www.neilsberg.com/insights/seattle-wa-median-household-income/

### Household Income Data
Neilsberg – Seattle Median Household Income: https://data.seattle.gov/dataset/Apartment-Market-Rent-Prices-by-Census-Tract/h27p-5k3i/about_data

---

## Methodology

1. Imported apartment rent and household income datasets from CSV files.
2. Cleaned and transformed the data using JavaScript.
3. Aggregated apartment rent values by year.
4. Calculated percentage growth relative to the 2018 baseline.
5. Built an interactive D3.js line chart comparing rent growth and income growth.
6. Organized the analysis into three periods:
   - Before COVID (2018–2019)
   - During COVID (2020–2021)
   - After COVID (2022–2023)

---

## Key Findings

- Both apartment rents and household incomes increased between 2018 and 2023.
- Median household income increased by approximately 17.1%.
- Apartment rents increased by approximately 10.7%.
- Rent growth slowed during the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic before increasing again in later years.
- Although income growth outpaced rent growth during this period, housing affordability remains a concern because overall housing costs remain high.
