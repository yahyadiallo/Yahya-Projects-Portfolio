Seattle Rent and Household Income Growth Before, During, and After COVID

Seattle has experienced rising housing costs and income growth over the past several years. This visualization compares average apartment rent growth and median household income growth from 2018 to 2023 to examine whether incomes have increased enough to keep up with changing housing costs before, during, and after COVID.

Results

The visualization shows that both average apartment rent and median household income increased between 2018 and 2023. However, household income grew at a faster rate than average apartment rent over the same period.

Before COVID: Rent and household income both increased between 2018 and 2019.

During COVID: Rent growth slowed around 2020 while household income continued increasing. This created a larger gap between the two trends.

After COVID: Both rent and income continued to rise, but household income showed a larger overall increase compared to 2018.

Takeaway: Household income grew faster than average apartment rent between 2018 and 2023, but housing costs remained higher after COVID than before the pandemic.

Sources:
Median household income data: Neilsberg, Seattle WA Median Household Income
Apartment rent data: Seattle Open Data, Apartment Market Rent Prices by Census Tract
